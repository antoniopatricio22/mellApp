Heart Attack Dataset

8,763 patients

The database used in this analysis gathers clinical information from 8,763 patients, with the goal of identifying factors that increase the risk of heart disease.

The artificial intelligence model was trained with the following variables:

Age
Gender (sex)
Chest pain type (cp)
Resting blood pressure (trtbps)
Serum cholesterol (chol)
Maximum heart rate (thalachh)
To simplify the experience during the first consultation, we chose to use only the most accessible and easy-to-provide data.

It's worth noting that the database used is already balanced, containing equivalent proportions between patients with and without elevated cardiac risk.
During testing, we found that it was not necessary to apply artificial balancing techniques, such as SMOTE, which contributes to an analysis more faithful to the reality of the original data.

The more active, the lower the risk

The chart on the side shows that people with higher levels of physical activity have a lower probability of suffering a heart attack.
This occurs because regular exercise strengthens the heart, improves circulation, and helps control risk factors such as obesity, diabetes, and hypertension.

Staying active is one of the most effective forms of prevention.

Age and Risk

The chart on the side shows that the risk of heart attack tends to increase with age. Most patients are concentrated between 50 and 65 years old, an age range where heart health care should be doubled.
